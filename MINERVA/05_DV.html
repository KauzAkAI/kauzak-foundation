Used 45 equipment and devices shall not be reused unless approved by the building official. 46 47 The Building Official Approval 48 49 If the Building Official is comfortable with the information provided, the BO may approve the use. 50 51 If the Building Official is not familiar with the product, is not comfortable with the design or calculations, 52 then 53 54 The Building Official can go to Step 2 and/ or 3. 55 56 57 The Second Step 58 1 The Building Official can request information documenting the product, the design, test information or 2 other information related to the product. 3 4 This information may be a test report from a recognized test lab. 5 6 These reports are items such as a Miami Dade Notice of Acceptance (NOA), an ICC Evaluation Services 7 (ICC ES) report, a Texas Department of Insurance (TDI) report, or 8 9 A rational Analysis of a previous or similar report about the product and the specific installation from an 10 expert, such as a Professional Engineer. 11 12 Most Building Officials are not experts in every field. 13 14 Most Building Officials are not practicing engineers. 15 16 Documentation and verification of products and their performance is generally required. 17 18 This information is readily available from the various testing entities and manufacturers. 19 20 Let us look at the statutory wording. 21 22 553.8425 - Local Product Approval. 23 24 1) For local product approval, products or systems of construction shall demonstrate compliance with 25 the structural windload requirements of the Florida Building Code through one of the following 26 methods: 27 28 a) A certification mark, listing, or label from a commission-approved certification agency indicating 29 that the product complies with the code; 30 31 b) A test report from a commission-approved testing laboratory indicating that the product tested 32 complies with the code; 33 34 c) A product-evaluation report based upon testing, comparative or rational analysis, or a 35 combination thereof, from a commission-approved product evaluation entity which indicates that 36 the product evaluated complies with the code; 37 38 d) A product-evaluation report or certification based upon testing or comparative or rational analysis, 39 or a combination thereof, developed and signed and sealed by a Florida professional engineer or 40 Florida registered architect, which indicates that the product complies with the code; 41 42 e) A statewide product approval issued by the Florida Building Commission; or 43 44 f) Designation of compliance with a prescriptive, material standard adopted by the commission by 45 rule under s. 553.842(15). 46 47 The Third Step 48 49 If Other Options Are Not Available - The third step is the Florida Product Approval System. 50 51 This was established to have a central clearing house of products that meet the Florida Building Code for 52 specific installations. 53 54 An Important Note! 55 56 No matter which system is used, just because a product has a test sheet or product approval number, the 57 use of that product must still meet the specific criteria of the installation. 58 This relates to design pressures, impact rating, fire resistance and attachment methods applicable to the 59 installation. 60 61 553.842 - Product evaluation and approval 1 2 1) The commission shall adopt rules under ss. 120.536(1) and 120.54 to develop and implement a 3 product evaluation and approval system that applies statewide to operate in coordination with the 4 Florida Building Code. The commission may enter into contracts to provide for administration of the 5 product evaluation and approval system. The commission’s rules and any applicable contract may 6 provide that the payment of fees related to approvals be made directly to the administrator. Any fee 7 paid by a product manufacturer shall be used only for funding the product evaluation and approval 8 system. The product evaluation and approval system shall provide: 9 10 a) Appropriate promotion of innovation and new technologies. 11 12 b) Processing submittals of products from manufacturers in a timely manner. 13 14 c) Independent, third-party qualified and accredited testing and laboratory facilities, product 15 evaluation entities, quality assurance agencies, certification agencies, and validation entities. 16 17 d) An easily accessible product acceptance list to entities subject to the Florida Building Code. 18 19 e) Development of stringent but reasonable testing criteria based upon existing consensus 20 standards, when available, for products. 21 22 f) Long-term approvals, where feasible.
