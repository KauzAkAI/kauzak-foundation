The refrigerant leaving the compressor is in the 57 gaseous state, and at a high pressure and temperature. This refrigerant then enters the condenser where 58 it loses the heat to the coolant that can be air or water. These are described below. 4 5 Air cooled condensers: Air cooled condensers are used in small units like household refrigerators, deep 6 freezers, water coolers, window air conditioners, split air conditioners, small-packaged air conditioners, 7 etc. These are used in plants where the cooling load is small and the total quantity of the refrigerant in the 8 refrigeration cycle is small. Air cooled condensers are also called coil condensers as they are usually 9 made of copper or aluminum coil. They occupy a comparatively larger space than water cooled 10 condensers. The two types of air-cooled condensers are natural convection and forced convection. 11 • In the natural convection type, the air flows over it in natural a way depending upon the 12 temperature of the condenser coil. 13 • In the forced air type, a fan operated by a motor blows air over the condenser coil. 14 15 Water cooled condensers: Water cooled condensers are used for large refrigerating plants, big, 16 packaged air conditioners, central air conditioning plants, etc. These are used in plants where cooling 17 loads are excessively high and a large quantity of refrigerant flows through the condenser. The three 18 types of water-cooled condensers are tube-in-tube or double pipe type, shell and coil type and shell and 19 tube type. In all these condensers the refrigerant flows through one side of the piping while the water 20 flows through the other side, cooling and condensing the refrigerant. 21 22 Evaporative condensers: Evaporative condensers are usually used in ice plants. They are a 23 combination of water cooled and air-cooled condensers. In these condensers, the hot refrigerant flows 24 through the coils. Water is sprayed over these coils. At the same time, the fan draws air from the bottom 25 side of the condenser and discharges it from the top side of the condenser. The spray water that comes 26 in contact with the condenser coil is evaporated, absorbs the heat from the condenser, and cools and 27 condenses the refrigerant. Evaporative condensers have the benefits of both water cooled and air-cooled 28 condensers, so they occupy less space. However, keeping the evaporative condensers clean and free of 29 scale is difficult and requires lots of maintenance. For this reason, they are not favored by HVAC 30 designers. 31 32 Ductwork 33 34 Ductwork channels conditioned air to vents, takes return air back into the system, and transports exhaust 35 air and waste heat to the exterior of a home. It’s literally split into two pieces. It is as referred to as “central air.” 5 6 Consisting of an outdoor unit with a condenser and compressor, and an indoor unit with a fan and an 7 evaporator coil, a split air conditioning system works by removing warm air from a home and cycling it 8 back as cooler air through a system of supply and return ducts. Powered by electricity, the compressor 9 pumps refrigerant through the system to collect heat and moisture inside the home. That heat and 10 moisture is removed from the home when the warm air collected inside is blown over the cooled indoor 11 coil that cools the air. The heat transferred into the coil during this process is pumped outside the home, 12 while the cool air is cycled back inside. 13 14 A split air conditioning system is particularly effective in warmer climates, as it effectively keeps homes 15 cool and reduces humidity levels. It also features a lower indoor noise level compared to free-standing air 16 conditioning units, since its compressor-bearing unit is outside of the home. Central air conditioning 17 systems, such as a split system, provide the added benefit of air filtration because, as a “forced air” 18 system, air is drawn out of rooms through air returns and pulled through an air filter. This removes 19 airborne particles and results in cleaner, filtered air being redistributed to the home through air supply 20 ductwork. 21 22 Transfer the Heat 23 24 Heat is the amount of energy transferred due to temperature difference between two bodies. HVAC 25 equipment transfers thermal (heat) energy from one location to another. 26 27 Heat flows from areas of high temperature to areas of low temperature, according to the Second Law of 28 Thermodynamics. The HVAC system is responsible for reversing this natural flow, removing hot air from 29 buildings in the hot summers and placing it in buildings in the cold winters. 30 31 How does that happen? It’s all about the heat exchanger. A heat exchanger is a device that transfers 32 thermal energy from one medium to another. 33 34 How does a freon system work? 35 36 Refrigerant is a general term that refers to a chemical blend inside an electro-mechanical air conditioner 37 that can shift easily between liquid and gaseous states. The earliest refrigerants were toxic or 38 combustible, which made air conditioning impractical outside of industrial buildings. But the invention of 39 the first non-toxic refrigerant blend, known by the trademarked name Freon, allowed air conditioners to 40 enter home use and spread across the country. The current standard refrigerant blend for residential air 41 conditioners is R-410A, sometimes called Puron, which produces fewer greenhouse gas emissions than 42 the previous blend, R-22. 43 44 As refrigerant transmutes from liquid to gas and back, it absorbs and releases heat. This is how it is 45 capable of cooling down a home. The compressor changes liquid refrigerant into a high-pressure, high- 46 temperature gas. The refrigerant moves to the outside coil of the air conditioner and releases its heat 47 there through condensation, cooling down. By the time it reaches the indoor coil, it has cooled down to 48 the point that it causes evaporation when it meets the warmer indoor air. This siphons heat from the air, 49 cooling it down. The refrigerant, once again a liquid, returns to the compressor to restart the cycle. 50 51 The refrigerant will remain at the same level for the life of the air conditioner unless leaks occur. If the 52 refrigerant level (its charge) starts to drop, professionals need to repair the system as soon as possible. 53 Low refrigerant will not only lower the cooling ability of the AC, but it can also severely damage the 54 compressor. 55 56 Section 1401 General 57 58 1401.1 Scope 59 60 This chapter shall govern the design, construction, installation, alteration, and repair of systems, 1 equipment, and appliances intended to utilize solar energy for space heating or cooling, domestic hot 2 water heating, swimming pool heating, or process heating. 3 4 1401.2 Potable Water Supply 5 6 Potable water supplies to solar systems shall be protected against contamination in accordance with the 7 Florida Building Code, Plumbing. 8 9 Exception: Where all solar system piping is a part of the potable water distribution system, in accordance 10 with the requirements of the Florida Building Code, Plumbing, and all components of the piping system 11 are listed for potable water use, cross-connection protection measures shall not be required. 12 13 1401.3 Heat Exchangers 14 15 Heat exchangers used in domestic water-heating systems shall be approved for the intended use.
