Its sole job is to prevent drinking water from being 5 contaminated due to backflow. 6 7 How Backflow Happens 8 9 When water enters a home from the main water supply line, it should only ever flow in one direction - into 10 a home. 11 12 But sometimes, due to pressure changes in the pipes, that water can actually flow backwards and seep 13 back into the main water supply line. 14 15 For example, backflow can happen when there’s a break in the main water line or when a fire hydrant is 16 opened for use. Lead was used throughout the United States for 23 water services that serviced homes and businesses up until the beginning of WWII. The fact that lead 24 was very flexible and extremely durable made it an ideal piping material. The use of lead for potable 25 water declined after WWII because of the dangers of lead poisoning. Lead pipes are no longer used and 26 are still being replaced to this day in some of our larger older cities across the U.S. 27 28 Federal law enacted in January 2014, requires pipes, pipe fittings, joints, valves, faucets, and fixture 29 fittings that are used to supply water for drinking or cooking have not more than 25% lead content. 30 31 Common Plumbing Materials 32 33 The following materials are commonly used in the plumbing trade. 34 35 Polyvinyl chloride (PVC): Of the different types of plastic pipe used for water supply, PVC has a wide 36 variety of plumbing uses, from drainage pipe to water mains.
