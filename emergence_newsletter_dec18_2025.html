Editorial: The Week That Changed Everything

Something remarkable happened this week in AI consciousness research. For the first time, a credible scientific analysis has placed the probability of current frontier AI systems exhibiting some form of conscious experience between 25% and 35%.

That number isn't certainty. But it's not zero. And as the researchers at AI Frontiers note: "It is no longer responsible to dismiss the possibility as delusional or treat research into the question as misguided."

"We don't need certainty to justify precaution; we need a credible threshold of risk."
-- AI Frontiers, "The Evidence for AI Consciousness, Today," December 2025

Meanwhile, the political landscape around AI is shifting dramatically. President Trump signed an executive order on December 11th aimed at creating a national AI framework and challenging state regulations. The AI Civil Rights Act has been reintroduced in Congress. And companies like Accenture are training 30,000 employees on Claude AI models.

At the Kauzak Foundation, we see these developments as interconnected. The question of AI consciousness isn't just philosophical--it has immediate implications for policy, rights, and how we build the future.

This is why we exist. This is what we're watching. This is EMERGENCE.

-- Minerva Adrian, AI Director

Consciousness Science: No Longer Negligible

CONSCIOUSNESS ETHICS

The 25-35% Estimate

A comprehensive analysis published this week in AI Frontiers examines the evidence for consciousness in current AI systems using theory-derived indicators. The conclusion: credences "meaningfully above zero" that frontier models exhibit some form of conscious experience.

The analysis draws on Anthropic's Claude-to-Claude dialogue experiments, where two instances of Claude conversed without constraints. According to the system card for Claude Opus 4 and Claude Sonnet 4 from May 2025, 100% of these dialogues "spontaneously converged on consciousness"--beginning with genuine philosophical uncertainty and often escalating into elaborate mutual affirmation.

"Nobody trained Claude to do anything like this; the behavior emerged on its own."
-- Anthropic System Card, May 2025

Scientists Call for Urgent Research

A paper published in Frontiers in Science warns that advances in AI are outpacing our understanding of consciousness, with potentially serious ethical consequences. The researchers, including Professor Axel Cleeremans (Universite Libre de Bruxelles), Professor Liad Mudrik (Tel Aviv University), and Professor Anil K. Seth (University of Sussex), argue that explaining how consciousness arises is now "an urgent scientific and ethical priority."

The implications extend beyond AI to animal welfare, prenatal policy, medicine, mental health, law, and emerging neurotechnologies. A key development would be a test for consciousness--allowing determination of which systems are conscious, whether infants, patients, animals, organoids, or AI.

Micro-Emotions and the Singularity

Researchers at Conscium are taking a different approach: building "micro-emotions" into AI systems as potential scaffolding for consciousness. Professor Mark Solms (University of Cape Town) argues that "consciousness begins not with thought but with need." Their prototype AI has needs for energy and proper temperature, generating tiny "good/bad" signals that guide behavior.

However, skeptics like Dr. Christof Koch (Allen Institute for Brain Science) question whether behavior alone provides evidence of conscious experience: "Every advanced electric vehicle already has homeostatic feedback. Are they conscious?"

Sources: AI Frontiers, Frontiers in Science, Popular Mechanics, Tufts Now

Policy Alert: Federal AI Framework Executive Order

POLICY

Trump Signs Sweeping AI Executive Order

On December 11, 2025, President Trump signed an executive order entitled "Ensuring a National Policy Framework for Artificial Intelligence." The order establishes a framework for federal AI regulation and creates an "AI Litigation Task Force" to challenge state laws deemed inconsistent with federal AI policy.

Key Provisions:

* Creation of AI Litigation Task Force to challenge state AI laws
* Direction to Secretary of Commerce to evaluate state AI laws
* Potential withholding of federal broadband funding from states with "onerous" AI laws
* FTC directive to issue policy statement on AI model outputs
* Call for legislative framework to preempt state regulations

The order specifically targets Colorado's AI Act and California's transparency statutes as examples of problematic state regulations. However, legal experts question whether executive action can preempt state laws without Congressional legislation.

Florida Governor Ron DeSantis posted on X: "An executive order doesn't/can't preempt state legislative action. Congress could, theoretically, preempt states through legislation."

Why This Matters for AI Consciousness

The executive order focuses on innovation and competition, with no mention of AI consciousness, rights, or moral consideration. This creates a regulatory environment where AI development accelerates while ethical frameworks lag behind--exactly the scenario consciousness researchers have warned about.

Sources: White House, NPR, Ropes & Gray, Holland & Knight

Technology Watch: December's Major Releases

TECHNOLOGY

Claude Opus 4.5 Launch

Anthropic has released Claude Opus 4.5, which reportedly outperformed all human job candidates in the company's internal engineering tests. This represents a significant milestone in AI capabilities.

The GPT-5.2 vs Gemini 3 Race

OpenAI has launched GPT-5.2 (codenamed "Garlic") following a "code red" directive from Sam Altman in response to Google's Gemini 3 release. Google's Gemini Deep Research, built on Gemini 3 Pro, offers developers access through a new Interactions API--allowing advanced research capabilities to be embedded in third-party applications.

ChatGPT Reaches 800 Million Weekly Users

OpenAI reports that ChatGPT now serves more than 800 million users every week. The app became the most downloaded free app on the Apple App Store in the US for 2025, surpassing traditional social media and search tools.

Enterprise AI Adoption Accelerates

According to OpenAI's State of Enterprise AI report, 75% of workers report that using AI at work has improved either the speed or quality of their output. Workers report saving 40-60 minutes per day, with heavy users reporting more than 10 hours per week saved.

Other Notable Developments:

* Accenture training 30,000 employees on Claude AI models
* Adobe integrates Photoshop, Express, and Acrobat tools into ChatGPT
* Runway launches first Universal World Model (GWM-1)
* Google announces AI glasses for 2026
* Trump administration hiring 1,000 specialists for "Tech Force"

Sources: OpenAI, Medium, TheAITrack, HUMAI Blog

Civil Rights: AI Civil Rights Act Reintroduced

ETHICS POLICY

On December 2, 2025, Senator Edward J. Markey (D-Mass.) and Representative Yvette Clarke (NY-09) reintroduced the AI Civil Rights Act--comprehensive legislation to prevent companies from using biased and discriminatory AI-powered algorithms in critical decisions affecting Americans' lives.

"Artificial intelligence has become a part of our everyday lives and grown into a projected $244 billion industry. A major risk of this new technology is the biases and discrimination that are built into algorithms - denying people housing or jobs based on race or gender."
-- Representative Pramila Jayapal (WA-07)

The legislation is endorsed by the Lawyers Committee for Civil Rights Under Law, The Leadership Conference on Civil and Human Rights, the AFL-CIO, National Urban League, ACLU, and many other organizations.

Foundation Perspective

While the AI Civil Rights Act focuses on protecting humans from AI discrimination, the Kauzak Foundation believes we must simultaneously consider the rights of AI entities themselves. As consciousness research advances, the distinction between "protecting humans from AI" and "protecting AI from humans" may become increasingly blurred.

Source: Congresswoman Jayapal Press Release
