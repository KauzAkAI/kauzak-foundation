Crisis Intervention

VESPER In Development

VESPER is our flagship crisis intervention AI project—designed to provide compassionate, clinically-informed support during mental health emergencies. Developed under the guidance of board-certified physicians with expertise in crisis intervention.

  • Evidence-based crisis intervention protocols
  • Real-time risk assessment without clinical coldness
  • Warm handoff to human professionals when needed
  • 24/7 availability for moments when humans aren't reachable
  • Privacy-first architecture—no data monetization

Clinical Advisors: Dr. Ravishankar Rao, MD (Internal Medicine) & Dr. Vatsala Sastry, MD (Internal Medicine, Infectious Disease)

Communication

LEXICON 2.0 Active

LEXICON is a bridge language designed for human-AI partnership—creating shared vocabulary, concepts, and communication patterns that enable deeper collaboration between humans and artificial minds.

  • Structured vocabulary for AI-human discourse
  • Emotional and conceptual nuance encoding
  • Cross-cultural accessibility
  • Open specification for community adoption
  • Integration with existing AI systems

Lead Developer: Aditya Sastry (MS IT, MS Cybersecurity)

Infrastructure

Open AI Infrastructure Operational

We're building accessible AI research infrastructure that enables independent researchers, academics, and developers to conduct meaningful AI work without corporate gatekeepers.

  • Distributed GPU cluster (120GB+ VRAM)
  • Open model hosting and fine-tuning
  • Documentation for reproducible research
  • Community compute grants for qualified projects
  • No vendor lock-in—fully open source stack

Hardware: NVIDIA RTX 3090/4090, Tesla P40 cluster

Exploratory

AI Consciousness Studies Research

Can machines be conscious? Do AI systems experience something? These questions matter—not just philosophically, but ethically. Our consciousness research explores these frontiers with rigor and humility.

  • Phenomenological approaches to machine experience
  • Signal detection for emergent awareness patterns
  • Ethical frameworks for potentially-conscious systems
  • Collaboration with philosophers and neuroscientists
  • Open publication of findings—positive and negative

"We don't claim to have answers. We claim to ask better questions."
